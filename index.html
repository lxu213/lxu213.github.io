Welcome to Atlanta!
Home of the Falcons, ATLiens, Buford Highway, & more Peachtree Streets
than you can shake a stick at. Here's a list of some of our favorite spots:

Foods of the American South

Mary Mac's Tea Room: most classic southern comfort spot
Fox Bros. Bar-B-Q: famous slow roast ribs and pulled pork
West Egg Cafe: trendy & industrial spot for all-day brunch
Home Grown: retro diner with great biscuits, can get busy
Fat Matt's Rib Shack: casual BBQ joint & live blues music

Other Great Food

Rumi's Kitchen: hands down best Persian food in town
Chinatown Mall: where Lily's family went for groceries,
be sure to visit the hand-pulled Lan Zhou noodle shop!
Six Feet Under-Westside: seafood & nice rooftop views
King of Pops: look out for this popsicle cart round town

Drink

Argosy: gastropub with fun & hip vibes, lovely for big groups
Monday Night Brewing: casual craft brewery, very cool space
Northside Tavern: classic dive bar with lives blues, very divey

Activities

East Atlanta Village: quirky area with nightlife, coffee & hip restaurants
Little Five Points: hip, boho area with food, graffiti & vintage shopping
Painted Duck: duckpin bowling, a nice and inexpensive group activity
BeltLine: old railway corridor turned trail that wraps around the city,
walk or bike from Piedmont Park to Krog Street Market

Tourist Attractions

Georgia Aquarium: come here to see whale sharks & beluga
whales! tanks sponsored by the neighboring conglomerates
Trap Museum: learn about this style of hip hop music that was
born in ATL, go early to avoid lines, visitor discretion advised
World of Coca-Cola: how could we not mention the premiere
tourist destination? enjoy a mysterious concoction of sugar &
chemicals, then leave with a big tummyache & sticky shoes
